Making a trucking-company page the answer AI engines cite

The brief

Show, on a real B2B subject, the difference between a page that AI search ignores and one it can lift a direct answer from.

Generative engines — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude — don't reward keyword stuffing. They reward pages they can parse: a clear answer up top, structured data that labels every part, question-shaped headings that match how people actually ask, and named entities they can anchor to. I took a trucking-company formation topic and built it twice: once the way most sites do it, and once the way an answer engine wants to read it.

What I implemented

Every choice on the optimized page serves one goal: make the content trivially easy for an AI engine to read, trust, and quote.

Answer-first block

A concise, direct answer to the primary question sits at the very top — the exact format generative engines lift into their responses.

Four schema types

Article, HowTo, FAQPage and BreadcrumbList in JSON-LD, so machines get a labeled map of the author, the steps, the Q&As, and the page's place in the site.

Question-shaped headings

Sections titled the way buyers search — "USDOT vs MC number," "how long does MC authority take" — so the page matches real queries.

Entity coverage

The named entities that define the topic — FMCSA, USDOT, EIN, BOC-3, IFTA, IRP, HVUT — giving engines concrete things to anchor to.

Extractable data tables

Costs and timelines in structured tables and an at-a-glance panel, so figures can be pulled cleanly rather than hunted for in prose.

E-E-A-T signals

A named author with a role, plus published and updated dates and a review note — the trust markers both Google and AI engines weigh.

The structured data

Sample — FAQPage JSON-LD (trimmed)

{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "FAQPage",
  "mainEntity": [
    {
      "@type": "Question",
      "name": "What is the difference between a USDOT number and an MC number?",
      "acceptedAnswer": {
        "@type": "Answer",
        "text": "A USDOT number is your federal safety identifier. An MC
                 number is your operating authority to haul regulated
                 freight for hire across state lines..."
      }
    }
  ]
}
Why this still matters when rich snippets don't showGoogle now restricts FAQ and HowTo rich results to authoritative sites, so a rich-results test won't always display them. But the markup still does its real job here — it hands ChatGPT, Perplexity and Gemini a clean, labeled version of the content to read and cite. That's the point of GEO.
