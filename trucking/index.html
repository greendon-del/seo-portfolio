02 · Your key question

How I measure AI search visibility

Straight answer: it's part direct testing, part analytics — because this space is still maturing. Here's the actual workflow.

Direct engine testing

I build the real questions your buyers ask and run them across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini and Claude, then check whether the site is cited or the brand mentioned. Perplexity and ChatGPT search expose their sources, so it's verifiable.

AI referral tracking

In GA4, traffic from chatgpt.com, perplexity.ai and similar now appears as its own source — so referral sessions from AI engines can be tracked directly over time.

Structured-data validation

I validate schema (validator.schema.org, Rich Results Test) as a leading indicator that the engines can parse the page cleanly and pull the right answer.

Share-of-voice trackers

For ongoing reporting I can layer in dedicated AI-visibility tools (Profound, Otterly) that monitor how often a brand surfaces across the models.

The nuance most people missGoogle now restricts FAQ and HowTo rich snippets to authoritative sites, so a rich-results test won't always light them up. But the structured data still does its real job — it hands the AI engines a clean, labeled version of the page to read and cite. That's the whole point of GEO.
