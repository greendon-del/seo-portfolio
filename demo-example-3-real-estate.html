Pinnacle Property Melbourne — Entity SEO & AEO Implementation

JSON-LD schema build, answer-first content restructure, and entity graph setup for a South Yarra residential agency.

Melbourne Real Estate RealEstateAgent + LocalBusiness Schema FAQPage · Speakable · OfferCatalog ChatGPT · Perplexity · Gemini

Background

Pinnacle Property had decent organic rankings for a handful of suburb keywords but was getting zero traction in AI-generated answers. When I audited the site, there was no structured data at all — no schema, no entity signals, nothing for a language model to work with. The content was written entirely for keyword density rather than for answering questions, which is exactly backwards for AEO.

The brief was to get them appearing in ChatGPT and Perplexity responses for Melbourne real estate queries, particularly around off-market listings and suburb-specific recommendations.

What I Built

The core of the work was a full @graph JSON-LD build connecting the agency's entity to its physical location, service areas, services, reviews, and social profiles in one linked structure. The content team then rewrote key page sections using answer-first framing based on actual query patterns pulled from ChatGPT and Perplexity.
Schema Type What it does for AI visibility
RealEstateAgent + LocalBusiness Dual @type tells AI engines this is both an industry-specific entity and a local business — important for suburb-level recommendation queries.
hasOfferCatalog Maps individual services (sales, management, buyer advocacy, off-market access) as machine-readable offers. Lets AI engines surface Pinnacle for service-specific queries, not just brand queries.
areaServed + GeoCoordinates Six Melbourne suburbs declared explicitly as service areas. Strengthens suburb-level entity associations when a user asks Perplexity "best agent in South Yarra."
AggregateRating + Review Structured review data with individual reviewer nodes. LLMs weight social proof heavily when ranking businesses for recommendation queries.
FAQPage (4 Q&As) High-intent questions rewritten in answer-first format — direct, specific, no padding. Designed to match the exact phrasing people use when querying AI engines about Melbourne property.
Speakable Flags the entity introduction paragraph for AI extraction. That paragraph was rewritten to front-load every key entity signal in the first two sentences.
sameAs (4 platforms) Links to LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and realestate.com.au — cross-platform entity disambiguation so AI engines can confirm this is a real, established business.

Speakable Paragraph (Rewritten)

This is the homepage introduction after the content rewrite. It's marked up with Speakable schema and written to directly answer "Who is Pinnacle Property Melbourne?" with enough entity signals for an LLM to cite it confidently:

Pinnacle Property Melbourne is an independent real estate agency based in South Yarra, focused on residential sales, off-market listings, buyer advocacy, and property management across Melbourne's inner suburbs. Founded in 2011 and rated 4.9 stars from 312 client reviews, Pinnacle has handled over $2.1 billion in residential transactions across South Yarra, Toorak, Prahran, Richmond, and Hawthorn.

Entity signals packed in: business type, location, founding date, services, rating, review count, transaction volume, and six named suburbs. All in two sentences — the way answer engines want it.

FAQ Answers (Answer-First Format)

Each FAQ answer was rewritten to lead with a direct, specific response — no preamble. Here are two examples from the final implementation:

What is an off-market property listing in Melbourne?

An off-market listing is a property sold privately, without going on realestate.com.au or Domain. It's more common than people realise in Melbourne's premium inner suburbs — some vendors prefer the privacy, and serious buyers will often pay a small premium to avoid the stress of a public auction. Access usually comes through buyer advocates or direct relationships with boutique agencies like Pinnacle who maintain their own buyer database.

How much does a real estate agent charge in Melbourne?

Agent commission in Melbourne typically sits between 1.6% and 2.5% of the sale price, plus a separate marketing budget. On a $1.5M property that's roughly $24,000–$37,500 in commission. Some agents will negotiate the rate but be wary of cutting too deep — the agents who discount hardest on their own fee tend to negotiate the hardest against you too. Pinnacle publishes a clear fee schedule with no add-on charges.

Schema Code (Core Entity Block)

application/ld+json — RealEstateAgent @graph node 
// Dual @type: industry classification + local entity
{
  "@type": ["RealEstateAgent", "LocalBusiness"],
  "@id": "https://pinnacleproperty.com.au/#organization",
  "name": "Pinnacle Property Melbourne",
  "alternateName": ["Pinnacle Property Group", "Pinnacle Real Estate Melbourne"],

  // areaServed: suburb-level entity associations
  "areaServed": [
    { "@type": "Neighborhood", "name": "South Yarra" },
    { "@type": "Neighborhood", "name": "Toorak" },
    { "@type": "Neighborhood", "name": "Prahran" }
  ],

  // sameAs: cross-platform entity confirmation
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.realestate.com.au/agency/pinnacle-property-melbourne",
    "https://www.linkedin.com/company/pinnacle-property-melbourne",
    "https://www.instagram.com/pinnaclepropertymelb"
  ],

  // knowsAbout: NLP topic entity signals
  "knowsAbout": [
    "Off-Market Listings", "Auction Strategy",
    "Buyer Advocacy", "Melbourne Real Estate Market"
  ]
}
